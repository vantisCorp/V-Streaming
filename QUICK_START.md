# V-Streaming - Quick Start Guide

## 🚀 Get Started in 5 Minutes

This guide will help you set up V-Streaming and start your first stream in just 5 minutes!

---

## 📋 Prerequisites

Before you begin, make sure you have:

- ✅ Windows 10 or 11 (64-bit)
- ✅ NVIDIA/AMD/Intel GPU with hardware encoding support
- ✅ 8GB RAM minimum (16GB recommended)
- ✅ Stable internet connection (5 Mbps upload minimum)
- ✅ Streaming account (Twitch, YouTube, Kick, etc.)

---

## 🎬 Step-by-Step Guide

### Step 1: Install V-Streaming (1 minute)

1. Download `V-Streaming-Setup.exe`
2. Run the installer as administrator
3. Follow the installation wizard
4. Launch V-Streaming

### Step 2: Complete Onboarding (2 minutes)

The first time you launch V-Streaming, you'll see the onboarding wizard:

1. **Welcome** - Click "Next"
2. **Interface Mode** - Choose "Simple" (beginner) or "Expert" (advanced)
3. **Capture Setup** - Select your capture source
4. **Audio Setup** - Select your microphone
5. **Scene Creation** - Create your first scene
6. **Streaming Setup** - Enter your stream key
7. **Keyboard Shortcuts** - Review shortcuts (optional)
8. **Tips and Tricks** - Read tips (optional)
9. **Completion** - Click "Start Streaming!"

### Step 3: Configure Your Stream (1 minute)

#### Add Capture Source
1. Click the **Capture** tab
2. Click **Add Capture Source**
3. Select **Game Capture** (for games) or **Window Capture** (for apps)
4. Choose your game or application
5. Click **Start Capture**

#### Set Up Audio
1. Click the **Audio** tab
2. Click **Add Audio Track**
3. Select your microphone
4. Adjust volume to 100%
5. Enable **Noise Gate** (optional)

#### Create Scene
1. Click the **Composition** tab
2. Click **Create Scene**
3. Name it "Gaming"
4. Add your capture source as a layer
5. Add your webcam as a layer (optional)
6. Arrange and resize layers

#### Configure Streaming
1. Click the **Streaming** tab
2. Select your platform (Twitch, YouTube, Kick, etc.)
3. Enter your stream key
4. Set **Bitrate**: 4500 kbps (recommended for 1080p 60fps)
5. Set **Resolution**: 1920x1080
6. Set **Frame Rate**: 60 fps
7. Select **Encoder**: NVENC (NVIDIA) or AMF (AMD)

### Step 4: Start Streaming! (1 minute)

1. Click **Start Streaming**
2. Wait for "LIVE" indicator
3. Check your stream on your platform
4. Interact with your chat
5. Click **Stop Streaming** when done

## 🔧 Troubleshooting

### Stream Won't Start

**Problem**: Clicking "Start Streaming" does nothing

**Solutions**:
1. Check your internet connection
2. Verify your stream key is correct
3. Try a different bitrate
4. Restart V-Streaming
5. Check firewall settings

### No Audio

**Problem**: Viewers can't hear you

**Solutions**:
1. Check your microphone is selected in Audio tab
2. Increase microphone volume
3. Check Windows sound settings
4. Test your microphone in Windows
5. Try a different microphone

### Black Screen

**Problem**: Capture source shows black screen

**Solutions**:
1. Make sure the game/application is running
2. Try running V-Streaming as administrator
3. Try Window Capture instead of Game Capture
4. Update your GPU drivers
5. Restart the game/application

### Dropped Frames

**Problem**: Stream is choppy or laggy

**Solutions**:
1. Lower your bitrate
2. Lower your resolution or frame rate
3. Close other applications
4. Check your internet upload speed
5. Use a wired connection instead of Wi-Fi

### High CPU Usage

**Problem**: V-Streaming uses too much CPU

**Solutions**:
1. Use hardware encoding (NVENC/AMF/QuickSync)
2. Lower your resolution or frame rate
3. Close other applications
4. Disable unnecessary effects
5. Update your GPU drivers

## 💡 Tips for Better Streams

### Audio Quality
- Use a good quality microphone
- Enable noise gate to reduce background noise
- Use headphones to prevent echo
- Test your audio before streaming
- Monitor audio levels during stream

### Video Quality
- Use hardware encoding for better performance
- Match your bitrate to your upload speed
- Use appropriate resolution for your content
- Keep your frame rate consistent
- Test different settings to find what works best

### Engagement
- Interact with your chat regularly
- Use overlays and alerts
- Set up interaction triggers
- Play mini-games during breaks
- Use multichat to see all chats

### Performance
- Close unnecessary applications
- Use wired internet connection
- Monitor your system resources
- Keep your drivers updated
- Use appropriate settings for your hardware

## 🎨 Customization

### Add Overlays
1. Click the **Composition** tab
2. Select your scene
3. Click **Add Layer**
4. Select **Image** or **Text**
5. Upload your overlay or add text
6. Position and resize

### Add Webcam
1. Click the **Capture** tab
2. Click **Add Capture Source**
3. Select **Webcam**
4. Choose your webcam
5. Click **Start Capture**
6. Add to scene in Composition tab

### Add Alerts
1. Click the **Interaction** tab
2. Click **Add Trigger**
3. Select trigger type (Follow, Subscribe, Donation)
4. Select action (Play Sound, Show Overlay)
5. Configure settings
6. Save trigger

### Use Filters
1. Click the **Composition** tab
2. Select a layer
3. Click **Add Filter**
4. Choose filter type (Color Correction, Sharpen, Blur, etc.)
5. Adjust settings
6. Apply filter

## 🚀 Advanced Features

### Multistreaming
1. Click the **Streaming** tab
2. Click **Add Target**
3. Select platform
4. Enter stream key
5. Repeat for additional platforms
6. Click **Start Multistreaming**

### VTubing
1. Click the **VTuber** tab
2. Click **Load Model**
3. Select .VRM or Live2D model
4. Click **Enable Face Tracking**
5. Adjust model settings
6. Add to scene

### AI Highlights
1. Click the **AI** tab
2. Enable **AI Highlights**
3. Set confidence threshold
4. Select highlight types
5. Start streaming
6. Review auto-clipped highlights

### Live Captions
1. Click the **AI** tab
2. Enable **Live Captions**
3. Select Whisper model size
4. Select language
5. Customize caption style
6. Start streaming
